1960 | Email

A method for exchanging electronic messages between computers. Created by several contributors, but Ray Tomlinson is considered the inventor of modern email.

1960s | First Online Game

Spaceware was the first online game invented by Rick Bloome in the 1960s, it was a pioneering space combat video game.

1970s | TCP/IP

TCP/IP are set protocols that define how data is transmitted over the internet, serving as the foundation for communication between different computer networks. Invented by Vint Cerf and Robert Kahn, American Computer Scientists.

1974 | The term "Internet"

The term "Internet" refers to a vast network composed of interconnected computer networks that facilitates communication and resource sharing on a global scale. The term most likely originated when the concept of interconnected networks began to develop in 1974.

1978 | Spam

Spam is unsolicited bulk electronic messages, this can be in the form of emails, text messages, phones calls, etc. Spam was first documented in 1978 when Gary Thuerk was marketing for a new computer model.

1980s | Online Banking

Online Banking was introduced in the 1980s, banking servicers started using online banking allowing customers to manage their finances from anywhere with a computer and an internet connection.

1980s | Wi-Fi

The concept of Wi-Fi was created by NCR Corporation, and it was standardized by Wi-Fi Alliance. Wireless networking technology that uses radio waves to connect devices to the internet.

1989 | WWW (World Wide Web) and Internet in Australia

WWW, also known as World Wide Web, is a system of interlinked hypertext documents accessed via the internet. It was established in 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee (British Computer Scientist).

Established in 1989 by a collaboration of government initiatives and telecommunications companies internet was introduced in Australia in the late 1980s, thanks to partnerships between universities and organizations like AARNet (Australian Academic and Research Network)

1990s | Broadband and Online Shopping

Broadband is high-speed internet access compared to tradition dial-up connections. It was established in the 1990s by the development of various technologies.

Online Shopping is the act of purchasing and selling products online through website or mobile applications.

1997 | Wi-Fi

Wireless networking technology (Wi-Fi) uses radio waves to connect devices to the internet. The concept was created by NCR Corporation in 1982. And it was available as a commerce product in late 1997.

1998 | Google

Google was original just a search engine and technology company offering various internet products. Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin, the both went to Stanford University as students and studied computer science.

2004 | Facebook

Facebook is a social networking platform created to connect people and enabling online communities. Founded by Mark Zuckerberg and Eduaro Saverin in 2004.

2005 | YouTube

YouTube was originally designed to be a dating website first created in 2005 but was changed to what it is today, a video sharing platform so users to upload there one content and share it with the world. They changed from a Dating site to a video sharing platform in April 2005. YouTube was created by Chad Hurley, Steven Chen, and Jawed Karim (these were all former PayPal employees).

2007 | NBN (National Broadband Network)

NBN (otherwise known as National Broadband Network) was established in 2007 and construction started in 2010, NBN was a result of a government initiative. It was created to provide a fast internet service to Australians.

2011 | Snapchat

Snapchat founded in 2011 by Evan Spiegel and Bobby Murphy, both Stanford University students. Snapchat is a multimedia messaging app known for it's disappearing photos and videos.

2016 | TikTok

TikTok is a short-form video platform that allows users to create and share engaging content. Launched in 2016, it was only available is China, but an international version was released in 2017.
