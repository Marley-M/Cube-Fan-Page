1960 | Email

A method for exchanging electronic messages between computers. Created by several contributors, but Ray Tomlinson is considered the inventor of modern email.

1974 | The term "Internet"

The term "Internet" refers to a vast network composed of interconnected computer networks that facilitates communication and resource sharing on a global scale. The term most likely originated when the concept of interconnected networks began to develop in 1974.

1978 | Spam

Spam is unsolicited bulk electronic messages, this can be in the form of emails, text messages, phones calls, etc. Spam was first documented in 1978 when Gary Thuerk was marketing for a new computer model.

1980s | Online Banking

Online Banking was introduced in the 1980s, banking servicers started using online banking allowing customers to manage their finacnes from anywhere with a computer and an interent connection.

1989 | WWW (World Wide Web)

WWW, also known as World Wide Web, is a system of interlinked hypertext documents accessed via the internet. It was established in 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee (British Computer Scientist).

1990s | Broadband and Online Shopping

Broadband is high-speed interenet access compared to traditiona dial-up connections. It was established in the 1990s by the development of various technologies.

Online Shopping is the act of purchasing and selling products online throufh website or mobile applications.

1997 | Wi-Fi

Wireless networking technology (Wi-Fi) uses radio waves to connect devices to the internet. The concept was created by NCR Corporation in 1982. and it was available as a commercle product in late 1997.

1998 | Google

Google was origianl just a search engine and technology company offering various internet products. Google was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin, the both went to Stanford University as students and studied copmuter science.

2004 | Facebook

Facebook is a social networking platform created to connect people and enabling online communities. Founded by Mark Zuckerberg and Eduaro Saverin in 2004.

2005 | YouTube

YouTube was origianlly designed to be a dating website first created in 2005 but was changed to what it is today, a video sharing platform so users to upload there one content and share it with the world. They changed from a Dating site to a video sharing platform in April of 2005. YouTube was created by Chad Hurley, Steven Chen, and Jawed Karim (these were all former PayPal employees).

2007 | NBN

NBN (otherwise known as National Broadband Network) was established in 2007 and construction started in 2010, NBN was a result of a government inititive. It was created to provide a fast internet service to Australians.

2011 | SnapChat

SnapChat founded in 2011 by Evan Spiegel and Bobby Murphy, both Stanford University students. SnapChat is a multimedia messaging app known for it's disappearing photos and videos.

2016 | TikTok

TikTok is a short-form video platform that allows users to create and share engaging content. Launched in 2016, it was only available is China but an international version was released in 2017.
